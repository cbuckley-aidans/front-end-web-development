Getting Started with Wireframing

Wireframing is the blueprint of your digital design. It's where we plan the basic structure before adding colors, images, and other visual elements.

Why Wireframe?

Wireframes help us focus on structure and user flow without getting distracted by visual design elements.

Explore UI Layout Basics

These sources explore mobile-specific designs but can also be applied to desktop designs as well. It is always best to design with mobile in mind first. Frameworks like Tailwind CSS and DaisyUI will often automatically handle mobile formatting for us, to ensure our designs run on all devices.

Paper Prototyping: Where Ideas Begin

Key Benefits

  • Quick visualization of ideas
  • Easy to make changes
  • Focus on structure and flow
  • Low cost, high impact

Pro Tips

  • Use basic shapes for elements
  • Include notes for interactions
  • Test with real users early
  • Iterate based on feedback

Digital Alternatives

  • Adobe XD
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Wireframe CC (Browser app, no account needed)
